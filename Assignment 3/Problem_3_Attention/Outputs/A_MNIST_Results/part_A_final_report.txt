Insights and observations you gained from your experiments:
- Adding Batch Normalization and Dropout was extremely necessary to stabilize the training curve. Without them, the models suffered from severe zigzagging and erratic learning due to overfitting.
- The computational cost is evident; the Attention mechanism consistently requires more training time due to the extra Conv2D and Pooling operations required to calculate the Spatial Mask.

Suggestions for future improvements, such as trying different types of attention or tuning the model further:
- For datasets like MNIST, hard Attention mechanisms (cropping the image based on bounding boxes) might be more effective than soft, continuous spatial masks.
- Experimenting with Channel Attention (e.g., SENet) instead of Spatial Attention might yield different results, focusing on 'what' convolutional filters are most important rather than 'where' the pixels are.
